The Curious Cat

In the quiet corner of an old library, tucked behind dusty shelves and forgotten scrolls, sat a peculiar book with a shimmering leather cover. No title, no author-just a faint golden glow that pulled like a heartbeat. One rainy afternoon, a curious girl named Mira wandered into the library, seeking shelter from the storm. As she explored the aim aisles, something called to her-a soft whisper, like a breeze through ancient trees. "Welcome, Reader," it wrote across the page. Startled, Mira stepped back. But the words kept appearing: "Within me are stories not yet told.Speak, and I shall show." Heart pounding, she whispered, "Show me the world of dragons." The ink swirled. Pages fluttered. In a blink, Mira was no longer in the library but standing atop a mountain, watching dragons dance in the sky. Every time she read, the book transported her into a new adventure-deep oceans, enchanted forests, starry kingdoms. But there was one rule: Never stop reading. One day, Mira closed the book mid-story. The library faded. The magic stilled. The book shut tight. But sometimes, when the night is quiet and the wind is just right, you can still hear its pages whispering...
